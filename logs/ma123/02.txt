I keep trying to build up intuition for how this problem gets solved. Each time I think of things slightly differently. Going to articulate what went through my mind when I attempted it this time.
---
Expression under a square root. With real numbers, when this expression is less than 0, it is undefined.
So, when is this expression less than 0?
---
Examining the expression under the square root $3x^2 - 36$, when $3x^2$ evaluates to 0, the answer is $-36$. In fact, any number smaller than 36 is going to produce a negative number, which means that $0 < 3x^2 < 36$, which means $0 < x^2 < 12$, and $0 < \sqrt{x^2} < \sqrt{12}$, or $0 < |x| < 2\sqrt{3}$. This is the interval that is not continuous. This can then be translated to the interval notation.
