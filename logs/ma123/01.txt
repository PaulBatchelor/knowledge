#! p 2.5.78
This was the one that I had the most trouble
wrapping my head around. My trouble spots were
grokking absolute value, and getting vertical asymptotes.
---
I'm going to say a few words about the vertical asymptotes. The numerator in the expression has an absolute value of $|98 - 2x^2|$, which means the expression inside will be negated if it is to be evaluated to be less than 0.
---
So, when will $98-2x^2$ be less than 0? Whenever $2x^2 > 98$, or $x^2 > 49$, which means that $|x| > 7$, the numerator will be $-(98 - 2x^2)$.
---
Back to vertical asymptotes. $|98 - 2x^2| =
|2(7 - x)(7 + x)| = |2(7-x)(x+7)$. The $(x + 7)$ cancels out, and we are left with a single VA of $x = 0$.
---
Computing one-sided limits. It's very easily to lose track of the negation in $(-x + 7)$, using $(7 - x)$ is easier. I missed the negation and got the limits
flipped.
