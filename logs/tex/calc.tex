When the infinity is found for the second one, we divide by $-x^3$ instead of $x^3$.
\par

The rule goes "divide by the largest term in the denominator". Well, the largest term is $\sqrt{25x^6} = \sqrt{25}\sqrt{x^6}$, so really we are dividing by $\sqrt(x^6)$. This gets simplified to $x^3$, but there's a caveat here. $\sqrt(x^6) = |x^3|$ the same way $\sqrt{x^2} = |x|$. If $x > 0$, then $x^3$ as $x \to \infty$ will just be $x^3$. However, $|x^3| = -x^3$ when $x \to -\infty$. So, for finding the limit $x \to -\infty$, the numerator is still being divided by $\sqrt(x^6)$ like the rule, but now $\sqrt{x^6}=|x^3|=-x^3$.
